<Section icon={Target} title="Best Attribute Priorities for Fast Players">

The right attribute mix depends on whether you want a pure creator, a scoring guard, or a two-way wing. For most players, speed should lead, but it should not be the only stat you care about. Build a Hooper’s attribute system rewards balance inside a focused role, especially when you want a player that still performs under pressure.

A useful rule is simple: every point in speed should have a job. If it helps you blow by defenders, great. If it also improves transition defense or quick recovery, even better. But once your movement is strong enough, the next points should support your main offense.

| Attribute | Priority | Why It Matters | Don’t Neglect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Speed | Very High | Improves separation, transition, and recovery | Burst without control is easy to defend |
| Ball Handling | Very High | Lets speed convert into clean drives | Speed alone will not beat pressure |
| Shooting | High | Punishes defenders who overplay the drive | One-dimensional slashers get crowded |
| Finishing | High | Turns open lanes into points | Empty speed feels good but scores less |
| Passing | Medium-High | Helps guards survive doubles and traps | Good creators need an exit pass |
| Defense | Medium-High | Keeps fast players useful off the ball | Pure offense can be targeted |
| Physical Profile | Medium | Supports size, reach, and matchup fit | Bad body choices can limit the role |

<Callout type="info" title="Best Priority Rule">
Fast players usually perform better when speed, handling, and one scoring skill all rise together. One elite stat is weaker than three useful ones.
</Callout>

| Build Style | Attribute Core | Strength | Weak Spot |
|---|---|---|---|

| Shot-First Guard | Speed, shooting, handling | Punishes space and closeouts | Can struggle in traffic |

The safest speed build for most players is the one that still works when the first option is taken away. That means at least one backup answer: a pull-up, a pass, or a strong cut to the rim. A fast build with no backup can look great in highlights and still feel awkward in actual possession flow.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="How to Set Up the Build Step by Step">

If you want a speed build that feels polished, follow a structured setup instead of spending points at random. The draft and career flow reward players who know their identity early. Once your role is clear, the rest of the build becomes easier to tune.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose the main role">
    Decide whether your player will create, score, or defend first. A speed build needs one clear purpose, especially in guard and wing positions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lock the movement core">
    Put speed and ball control near the top of your early priorities. This gives you a playable base before you spend on secondary stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add one scoring weapon">
    Pick either shooting or finishing as your main way to punish defenders. Do not leave the build with only movement and no threat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Patch the weak point">
    Use remaining points to cover defense, passing, or physical stability. This keeps the build useful when opponents force you into uncomfortable plays.
  </Step>
</Steps>

|---|---|---|

| Do you want a simpler playstyle? | Choose a slashing guard | Choose a two-way wing |
