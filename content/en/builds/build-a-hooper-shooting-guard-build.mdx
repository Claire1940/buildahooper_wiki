<Section icon={Map} title="Build a Hooper Shooting Guard Build Overview">

A strong shooting guard should feel like a dependable scorer first and a useful defender second. That balance matters because SGs are often expected to punish space, attack closeouts, and still hold their own on the perimeter. If you try to do everything at once, the build usually feels average. If you lock in one clear identity, the player becomes much easier to pilot and upgrade.

<Callout type="tip" title="Core Identity">
Build for a clear job: shot creation, catch-and-shoot spacing, or two-way wing pressure. The best SG builds stay narrow enough to be efficient.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Best Body Setup and Role Fit">

Body settings should support the role you want to play, not fight it. For a shooting guard, that usually means protecting speed, preserving quick animations, and leaving room for a functional perimeter game. A taller frame can help with contests and rebounding, but it should not slow the build so much that you lose separation on offense.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not over-invest in size if your main value comes from perimeter scoring. Extra height can be helpful, but it should never erase your first-step advantage.
</Callout>

| Build Choice | Suggested Direction | Why It Works | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lean Scoring SG | Moderate height, lighter weight | Faster separation, easier shot creation | Less physical contact |
| Balanced Wing | Mid-height, balanced weight | Safer on both ends | Not elite in one area |
| Defensive SG | Slightly stronger frame | Better contests and bumps | Lower top-end quickness |
| Slashing SG | Agile frame with enough strength | Strong paint pressure | More careful shot selection |

| Stat Priority | Early Value | Mid Value | Late Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Speed | High | High | Medium |
| Acceleration | High | High | Medium |
| Perimeter Defense | Medium | High | High |
| Strength | Low | Medium | Medium |
| Rebounding | Low | Low | Medium |

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Fit">
If you want the most reliable all-around SG, aim for a frame that protects speed first, then add enough strength to stay playable on switches.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Attribute Allocation Priority">

Attribute spending is where most SG builds succeed or fail. The first priority is always the part of your game that creates repeatable offense. For most players, that means shooting, ball handling, and a base layer of speed. After that, defense and finishing should fill the gaps instead of trying to define the whole build.

<Callout type="info" title="Upgrade Order">
Spend points in the order you use them in real possessions: create space, convert the shot, protect the ball, then add defense.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lock in your scoring base">
    Upgrade the shots you will take the most. A shooting guard needs dependable perimeter scoring before anything flashy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add ball control">
    Raise handling enough to escape pressure and create a clean first move. That keeps your offense alive when the defense closes fast.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build the mobility package">
    Improve speed and acceleration so your player can separate, recover, and chase better angles on both ends.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Fill in defense and finishing">
    Use the remaining points on perimeter defense, steals, and finishing tools that match your chosen style.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Attribute | Priority | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Shooting | Very High | Main source of SG value |
| Ball Handling | Very High | Creates self-generated looks |
| Speed | Very High | Helps separation and recovery |
| Acceleration | High | Improves burst on first step |
| Perimeter Defense | High | Keeps you playable on switches |
| Steal | Medium | Adds pressure on the perimeter |
| Finishing | Medium | Rewards drives and closeouts |
| Strength | Medium | Helps against contact and bumps |

<Callout type="success" title="Practical Rule">
If an attribute does not improve your next ten possessions, delay it. Efficient SG builds win through consistency, not random stat padding.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Badges, Defense, and Game-Day Habits">

Badges should amplify the identity of your SG, not distract from it. A scoring guard wants tools that make spacing easier, finishes cleaner, and pressure less predictable. At the same time, a modern shooting guard cannot ignore defense. Even a scoring-first build needs enough resistance to stay on the floor in tougher matchups.

<Callout type="success" title="Two-Way Advantage">
The best SG builds are hard to target. Even when scoring is the headline, defense should still be visible in your badge plan.
</Callout>
