<Section icon={Map} title="Build a Hooper Career Guide: Choose Your Role">

A strong career starts before the first season. The smartest approach is to define your job on the floor first, then spend attributes to support that identity. If you try to be great at everything, you usually end up average in the places that matter most.

The best early decision is whether you want to score, create, defend, or dominate inside. That choice shapes your position, your build, and your long-term progression path. For new players, role clarity is more valuable than chasing the flashiest stat line.

| Position | Best For | Primary Strengths | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Point Guard | Playmakers, ball handlers | Passing, tempo control, creation | Too little scoring pressure |
| Shooting Guard | Scorers, perimeter threats | Shot creation, spacing, finishing | Weak defense if ignored |
| Small Forward | Flexible two-way players | Balance, versatility, matchup coverage | Can feel diluted without focus |
| Center | Paint anchors, rebounders | Size, rim protection, interior scoring | Limited mobility if overbuilt |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Best Build Paths for Different Playstyles">

Once your role is set, the next step is choosing a build path that supports it. The goal is not to maximize every number. It is to create a player who performs well in the situations you actually face most often.

The table below is the simplest way to compare common playstyles. Use it to narrow your direction before you commit points.

| Build Path | Typical Positions | Priority Stats | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scoring Guard | PG, SG | Handling, shooting, burst | Moderate |
| Two-Way Wing | SG, SF | Perimeter defense, shooting, speed | Moderate |
| Balanced Creator | PG, SF | Passing, shooting, reaction time | Easier for beginners |
| Interior Anchor | C | Strength, rebounding, defense | Simple, but role-specific |

A balanced creator is usually the safest starter build because it gives you options in both offense and defense. That matters if you are still learning how the game rewards different actions over a full career.

On the other hand, a pure scorer can feel powerful early but may punish sloppy decision-making. If you want a more forgiving experience, lean toward a build that contributes in multiple phases of play without requiring perfect execution.

| Build Path | Strongest In | Weakest In | Who Should Use It |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scoring Guard | Shot creation | Interior contests | Confident perimeter players |
| Two-Way Wing | Matchups, transitions | Extreme specialization | Players who value balance |
| Balanced Creator | All-around impact | Elite peak output | New players and casual grinders |
| Interior Anchor | Paint control | Spacing and mobility | Rebound-first users |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not copy a highlight build without checking whether it fits your preferred pace. A great build on paper can feel awkward in real play.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Attribute Priorities That Shape Your Career">

Attributes should follow your role, not your mood. If you upgrade randomly, you make your future seasons harder than they need to be. Start with the stats that directly help your build win possessions.

Think in buckets. Offense creates scoring chances, defense keeps you on the floor, physicals determine how your player moves and survives contact, and passing or handling decides whether you control possessions. The right balance depends on your position, but the priority order should stay focused.

| Attribute Group | What It Affects | Early Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shooting | Consistency from range and midrange | High for guards and wings | Best when paired with good spacing |
| Finishing | Rim pressure and close scoring | High for slashers | Helps when your speed is strong |
| Passing / Handling | Playmaking, ball security, creation | High for point guards | Keeps possessions clean |
| Defense | Stops, contests, steals, boards | High for two-way players | Never ignore it completely |
| Physicals | Speed, strength, mobility | Medium to high | Supports every other category |

A simple priority chart can help you avoid wasted points. If you are building a guard, start with handling and shooting. If you are building a wing, make sure defense does not fall too far behind offense. If you are building a center, size and rebounding should stay near the top of the list.

| Playstyle | Priority 1 | Priority 2 | Priority 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scoring Guard | Handling | Shooting | Speed |
| Two-Way Wing | Defense | Shooting | Physicals |
| Balanced Creator | Passing | Shooting | Defense |
| Interior Big | Rebounding | Interior defense | Strength |

<Callout type="info" title="Upgrade Logic">
Spend points where they change possessions, not where they only make the build look complete. Useful stats beat empty balance.
</Callout>

</Section>
