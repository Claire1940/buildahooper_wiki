<Section icon={Target} title="Positions and Attribute Priorities">

The position you choose should set the tone for the rest of the build. Point guards want control, shooting guards want scoring pressure, small forwards want flexibility, and centers want presence inside. In this game style, position is not just a label. It should tell you where your points belong and what your player is expected to do every possession.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not build without a role in mind. If you split points evenly across every category, you may end up with a player that feels usable but never truly dangerous.
</Callout>

| Position | Role | Priority Attributes | Common Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Point Guard | Primary ball handler | Passing, speed, handling | Cleaner offense creation |
| Shooting Guard | Perimeter scorer | Shooting, movement, finishing | Reliable scoring pressure |
| Small Forward | Versatile wing | Balanced offense, defense, athleticism | Two-way flexibility |
| Center | Interior anchor | Size, rebounding, defense | Strong paint control |

A smart build also starts with attribute groups instead of isolated stats. Think in buckets: offense, defense, physicals, and balance. That makes it easier to stay consistent while you spend points. It also keeps your build aligned with the game’s career focus, where long-term development matters more than one flashy strength.

| Attribute Group | Best For | Recommended Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Offense | Scorers and creators | Shooting, finishing, ball control |
| Defense | Stopper builds | On-ball defense, interior defense |
| Physical | Power and mobility | Speed, size, athletic profile |
| Balanced | All-around players | Moderate strength in every area |

<Callout type="info" title="Practical Rule">
If you want your player to feel sharp early, stack the attributes that support your main role first, then use the remaining points to cover one weakness.
</Callout>
