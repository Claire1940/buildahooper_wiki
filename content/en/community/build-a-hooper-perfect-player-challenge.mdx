<Section icon={Target} title="Best Positions and Role Matchups">

Position choice shapes everything that comes next. In this mode, your player should feel natural on the court, not forced into a style that only works on paper. A good position makes your attributes feel stronger because every point supports a job you can actually repeat.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not copy a build just because it looks powerful. If you cannot consistently use the strengths tied to that position, the build will feel weaker than a simpler setup.
</Callout>

| Position | Best Use | Priority Stats | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Point Guard | Primary creation | Passing, handle, shooting | Thin defense if overbuilt offensively |
| Shooting Guard | Perimeter scoring | Shooting, speed, movement | Limited playmaking if ignored |
| Small Forward | Two-way versatility | Balanced offense, defense, athleticism | Can become average at everything |
| Center | Paint control | Rebounding, strength, interior defense | Slow offense if mobility is too low |

| Role Type | Best When You Want | Usually Safer For |
|---|---|---|
| Guard | Ball-heavy scoring | Players who like controlling possessions |
| Wing | Flexible impact | Players who want fewer hard weaknesses |

<Callout type="info" title="Position Rule of Thumb">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Attribute Allocation and Build Order">

A smart attribute split should always serve a job. Early points need to create usable strengths, not just inflate the overall shape of the build. The best player challenges are won by making a few stats reliable before trying to cover every category at once.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lock the Core Identity">
    Decide whether the build is a scorer, creator, defender, or big-man anchor. This choice should guide every later point allocation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Set Three Pillars">
    Pick one primary skill, one support skill, and one utility skill. For example, shooting, ball handling, and defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Raise the Weak Link Last">
    Improve survival stats only after your main job feels stable. That keeps the build from becoming spread too thin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Test and Adjust">
    After a few games, check where possessions break down. Fix the stat that hurts consistency the most.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Attribute Group | Early Target | Mid Target | Why It Helps |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shooting | Reliable make rate | Consistent shot creation | Raises scoring floor |
| Passing/Handle | Basic control | Pressure creation | Reduces turnovers |
| Defense | One strong stop tool | Better matchup control | Wins close possessions |
| Physicals | Speed or strength | Position-specific balance | Supports your main role |

<Callout type="success" title="Smart Spending">
Spend points where they change possession outcomes first. A build that can create clean looks, stay in front of opponents, or own the paint will feel much stronger than one with shallow upgrades everywhere.
</Callout>

| Build Priority | Best Use Case | Do Not Overinvest If |
|---|---|---|
| Shooting first | You want offense as your identity | You cannot create open looks yet |
| Defense first | You want high utility and consistency | Your offense becomes unusable |
| Physical first | Your role depends on size or pace | Your core skills are still weak |

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Common Mistakes, Fixes, and FAQ">

Most failed builds come from the same few errors: too many mixed priorities, weak defense, and overconfident early spending. Once you know the traps, the challenge becomes easier to control and much less frustrating.

| Mistake | Simple Fix |
|---|---|
| Stacking too many stats | Keep one primary skill and one support skill |
| Ignoring defense | Add at least one defensive package |
| Picking the wrong role | Match the build to how you actually play |
| Overloading physicals early | Upgrade them after core skills feel usable |

<Callout type="tip" title="Final Optimization">
If two builds look similar, choose the one with cleaner decision-making. The easier build to use usually performs better over a long run because it wastes fewer possessions.
</Callout>
